Schools Rules and Regulations


A. Awards and Recognition

    Assemblies are called for ceremonies, announcements, programs, contests, common activity periods, and the like. Students are expected to behave accordingly in all assemblies. The class adviser is responsible for the class whenever the activity requires the presence of the entire student group. During the general assembly. the Morning Prayer is said followed by the National Anthem.

B. Attendance

    A student is required to attend all classes and other school activities punctually and regularly from the first day of classes.
    The maximum number of permissible absences, either excused or unexcused, consists of 20% of the prescribed number of class periods.
        1. Students, after an absence, must present a letter of excuse duly signed by parent or guardian to the Administrative Office.
        2. Excuse slips for absences may be secured from the Administrative Office and should be presented to the class adviser before entering the classroom.
        3. A student is considered late after the flag ceremony has started. Three instances of tardiness is equivalent to 1 day of absence. This will be handled by the Adviser, and parents will be informed. A conference with the parent or guardian is required if the student continues to be late.

C. Calling Out from Classes

    Only the Principal or the School Director may call out a student from class. A permit to leave the classroom is filled out before a student is allowed to leave the classroom. A student who is taking a test may not be called out from class.

D. Permit to Leave the Classroom

    No student shall be allowed to leave the classroom without seeking a permit signed by the teacher.

E. Permit to Leave the School

    No student is allowed to leave the school premises during class hours or break period. A student can only do so if the parent or guardian will pick him/her up duly coordinated with the teacher/Adviser.

F. Reporting to the School After Illness

    In cases of prolonged absences, 3 days or more due to illness, a student must present a Doctor's Certificate and clearance indicating that the student is already safe to attend classes.

G. Permission to Leave the Infirmary

    In case a student is severely ill, the school informs the parent or guardian through the telephone or cell phone, so the student can be taken home. In case there is difficulty in contacting the parent or guardian of the student, he/she will be brought to the school clinic for immediate treatment.

H. Dismissal Time and Curfew Hour

    During dismissal, students must move out of the classroom orderly and quietly. The students are enjoined to go home at the soonest possible time. Students waiting for their fetcher or ride services must stay within their assigned classroom or designated area and must be accompanied by a member of the faculty or school personnel.

I. Suspension of Classes

    Suspension of classes due to natural calamities shall be governed by DepEd guidelines, and official pronouncement from DepEd Region IVA (Rizal) will be followed. However, localized situation is considered; therefore the announcement of suspension of classes is the responsibility of the School Director/ School Administrator.

J. System Communication

    Official announcements are made through the public address system. Written announcements, on the other hand, are posted on the bulletin boards. It is the responsibility of every student, parent, or guardian of students to be informed fully by reading regularly posted notices. Other important communications, in the form of circulars or notices, are written down in the handbook.

K. School Uniform, Haircut, and Use of I.D Card

    Wearing complete uniform properly speaks of good grooming, refinement, and culture in school among students. It certainly adds a sense of dignity, pride, and honor to the school and its community when the students follow the prescribed daily uniform complete with I.D., the prescribed haircut and shoes, as well as the P.E. uniforms.
School I.D. must be displayed and worn properly.
    Boys and Girls: hanging around the neck through the use of the official cord only.
Daily Uniforms
Boys:    Brown short pants (for preschool to Gr.3 pupils), brown long pants (for Gr.4 to Gr.6 students),              brown polo shirt with white collar and white sleeves, color coded neck tie, white undershirt;              black low-cut leather shoes with white socks.
Girls:    Brown navy collared A-line dress with color coded ribbon (for preschool pupils), white navy              collared blouse with brown lining, color coded ribbon (bow-tied ribbon for Gr.1-Gr.3 and              lambda for Gr.4-Gr.6), and brown pleated skirt.
P.E Uniform
Boys and Girls:White shirt with logo and red long jogging pants.
Prescribed Haircut
Boys:    Clean cut is the prescribed haircut. Colored hair is not allowed as well as to sport spiked hair,              and or braided hair.
Girls:    Go to school with well combed or fixed hair. Dyed colored hair is not allowed.

L. Use of School Name

    Students and other individuals and groups may not use the school name and/or school seal for any activity and on any printed materials (T-shirt, jackets, etc.) without explicit permission from school authority. This also applies to printed programs, invitations, announcements, tickets, parties, or other similar activities or projects.

M. Guidelines on Visitors

    - Visitors are always welcome. However, no one is allowed to visit the classrooms during school hours. Parents or Guardians who wish to talk to the teachers have to make an appointment through the Administrative Office.
    - Bringing food for some occasions, like birthdays, is allowed, provided, that the teacher or adviser is informed beforehand and must follow only the allowed time.
    - Wear decent attire when inside the school. Please wait for your children on the designated area.     - Smoking is strictly prohibited. Please observe cleanliness and peace and order inside the school premises.

N. Care and USe of School Property/Facilities

    All students are responsible for keeping the classroom and immediate surroundings neat and orderly at all times. Students are expected to use school furniture and equipment with care. Any culpable damages done by the student to school properties shall be charged to his account. If responsibility cannot be pinpointed, the whole class will be held responsible.
